Classic Jollof Rice

A smoky, tomato-rich rice dish loved across West Africa. It’s weeknight-friendly and crowd-pleasing—don’t skip the gentle simmer where the flavor develops.

Recipe Information

Prep time: 20 minutes
Cook time: 45 minutes
Servings: 6
Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice, rinsed until water runs clear
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-size piece ginger, grated
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups blended tomatoes & red bell peppers (tomato-pepper mix)
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1–2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp paprika or cayenne (to taste)
  • 2–2½ cups chicken stock
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté onions until soft; add garlic and ginger for 30 seconds.
  2. Stir in tomato paste and cook 2–3 minutes until it darkens slightly—this reduces sourness.
  3. Add the tomato-pepper mix, thyme, curry, bay leaves, and paprika. Simmer 10–12 minutes, stirring to prevent burning.
  4. Add rinsed rice; stir to coat. Pour in stock to just barely cover the rice. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cover tightly. Cook on low heat 25–30 minutes until rice is tender; add a splash of stock if needed.
  6. Fluff with a fork; fold in vegetables if using. Cover and steam 5 minutes more. Adjust seasoning and serve.

Tips

  • For a signature smoky note, let the bottom toast lightly (not burn).
  • Parboiled long-grain rice helps prevent mushiness.
  • Keep the lid sealed while cooking to trap steam.

Allergy note: Swap in vegetable stock for a vegetarian version.
